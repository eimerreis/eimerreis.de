## Design Context

### Users
- Mixed audience: peers/creatives, clients or hiring teams, and friends/community.
- Primary context: visitors land to understand who Moritz is and what he builds, then branch into writing or playlists.
- Core job to be done: quickly build a strong sense of the person, perspective, and craft behind the work.

### Brand Personality
- 3-word personality: bold, artistic, experimental.
- Voice and tone: warm confidence first, playful delight throughout, with selective moments of urgency and momentum.
- Emotional goals: feel unmistakably human, expressive, and intentional rather than neutral or generic.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Keep both light and dark themes, anchored in the existing warm paper + vibrant accent palette.
- Lean into indie-portfolio and music-culture references: strong typography, layered surfaces, rhythm in spacing, and dynamic composition.
- Preserve the current editorial art direction while pushing for more personality in key moments.
- Anti-direction: avoid starter-template aesthetics, generic SaaS minimalism, and interchangeable layouts.

### Design Principles
- Lead with identity: every key page should help visitors understand the person behind the work within the first viewport.
- Be bold with purpose: expressive visuals should communicate intent, never decoration without meaning.
- Keep editorial clarity: maintain strong hierarchy, readable measure, and consistent content rhythm.
- Build inclusive polish: target WCAG AA, preserve reduced-motion support, and maintain accessible contrast in both themes.
- Design for memorability: prefer distinctive components and composition choices over generic patterns.
